The problem
Your deal structure
is your negotiating leverage.
When multiple parties share ownership of a creative work — a song, a film score, a licensed IP — they need to agree on how revenue gets split. Putting that agreement on a public blockchain exposes it to everyone: competitors, platforms, and other rights-holders can see exactly how the deal is structured and use that information against you.

RoyaltyLayer solves this. Each rights-holder encrypts their percentage share locally in their browser before it ever touches the blockchain. The contract verifies that all shares add up to 100% — without ever seeing a single individual value. Revenue distributes proportionally. Each party claims and decrypts only their own amount. No one else sees what you received.

What it does
Six functions.
All private.
01
Encrypted split registration
Each rights-holder encrypts their BPS share locally before it touches the chain. The contract stores only ciphertext.
02
On-chain sum enforcement
The KMS verifies that encrypted splits sum to 10,000 BPS and returns a cryptographic proof. No individual value is revealed.
03
Private revenue claims
Revenue distributes proportionally using FHE arithmetic. Each party decrypts only their own share.
04
Auditor access control
Grant a trusted observer read access to your specific share only. Explicit, on-chain, initiated by you alone.
05
Independent verification
Every claim is verifiable on Sepolia. Gas, transaction hashes, and asset states are public. Split values stay private.
06
No trusted operator
No admin key, no backend, no platform that can alter your split or intercept your claim. The protocol is the operator.
Protocol participants
Who is involved
Rights Holder
An artist, songwriter, or producer with an ownership stake. Registers their encrypted share and claims revenue privately.
Deal Registrant
The party who initiates asset registration — typically one of the rights-holders. Coordinates the initial split setup.
Distributor
A label or platform that deposits revenue into the contract without knowing how it will be divided.
Observer
A trusted third party — accountant, legal counsel — granted read-only access to one holder's share explicitly.
KMS
Zama's Key Management Service. Verifies encrypted splits sum to 10,000 BPS. Never sees individual values.
Contract
On-chain logic enforcing all rules — validation, state transitions, revenue accounting. Operates entirely on encrypted data.
